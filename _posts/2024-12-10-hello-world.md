Like many Korean-Christian parents, my mother was always inspired by the biblical figure of Daniel, who served as an interpreter of dreams. I was even named after him, with the hope that I would also grow up to be someone who could interpret complex ideas and solve challenging problems.

Originally, I pursued this dream by studying accounting and finance, with the goal of becoming a financial analyst. But as I progressed in my studies, I began to realize that my true passion was not in accounting, but rather in the world of data science.

Data science is a rapidly growing field that uses scientific methods, processes, algorithms, and systems to extract knowledge and insights from structured and unstructured data. It is a field that has the potential to impact almost every aspect of our lives, from healthcare and finance, to transportation and retail.

As someone who has always been fascinated by the power of data and its ability to drive decision-making and progress, I knew that I wanted to be a part of this exciting field. And so, I made the decision to transition from my Accounting degree to a combined major in Commerce and Computer Science, with the goal on becoming a data scientist.

This transition has not been without its challenges, but I am confident that it is the right decision for me. I am excited to learn more about data science and to develop the skills and expertise that will allow me to become an interpreter of data, just like the biblical Daniel was an interpreter of dreams.

By starting this blog, I am hoping to keep myself accountable in my daily practice and self-learning. I look forward to sharing my experiences and insights as I continue to learn and grow in the field of data science.
